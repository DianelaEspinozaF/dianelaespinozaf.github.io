Hi! I'm Dianela, an economist from Chiclayo, Peru, currently pursuing a [Master's in Data Science and Public Policy](https://www.lse.ac.uk/study-at-lse/Graduate/degree-programmes-2024/MPA-Data-Science-for-Public-Policy) at the London School of Economics (LSE) as a [winner of the Peruvian Scholarship for Public Service](https://www.lse.ac.uk/school-of-public-policy/news/students/Peruscholarship).

My research focuses on using rigorous quantitative methods to evaluate social programs and understand educational inequalities. I am particularly interested in education, early childhood development, conditional cash transfers, and the role of data science in informing evidence-based policy in developing countries.

Before LSE, I worked as a Research Assistant at [GRADE](https://www.grade.org.pe/) on the [Young Lives](https://www.younglives.org.uk/) longitudinal study ([Niños del Milenio](https://www.ninosdelmilenio.org/) in Peru), and as a Research Intern at [Innovations for Poverty Action](https://poverty-action.org/), where I contributed to projects on gender gaps in STEM and intercultural bilingual education.

I am passionate about continuing work in education because education changed my life. I have the conviction that access to quality education should be a right, not a privilege.

## Research in Progress

**Long-term Effects of Conditional Cash Transfers on Human Capital and Family Formation**
*with Alan Sanchez (University of Oxford) and Amanda Woodman (University of Oxford)*

I evaluate whether Peru's conditional cash transfer programme (JUNTOS) impacts long-term human capital and family formation outcomes using propensity score matching on longitudinal data (2006-2023) from 2,000+ Young Lives participants.
